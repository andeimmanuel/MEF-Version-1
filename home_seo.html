Our Journey of Hope

The Mary Emmanuel Foundation was established in 2010 by Dr. Mary Emmanuel, a pediatrician who witnessed firsthand the devastating effects of inadequate healthcare and education in underserved communities. What began as a small medical outreach program in rural Kenya has grown into a global initiative spanning three continents.

Our mission is to create sustainable solutions that empower communities to break the cycle of poverty through access to quality education, healthcare, advocacy, and economic empowerment programs. We believe that every individual deserves the opportunity to reach their full potential, regardless of their circumstances.

Over the past 15 years, we have established 23 schools, 12 healthcare centers, and numerous community empowerment programs that have directly impacted over 500,000 lives. Our holistic approach addresses the interconnected challenges facing underserved communities, creating lasting change that spans generations.

Our Programs

We take a holistic approach to community development through four key program areas that work together to create sustainable change.

Education

Providing access to quality education through school construction, teacher training, scholarship programs, and educational resources.

Healthcare

Delivering essential medical services, preventive care, maternal and child health programs, and health education initiatives.

Advocacy

Amplifying community voices, promoting policy change, protecting human rights, and raising awareness about critical issues.

Empowerment

Building economic resilience through vocational training, microfinance initiatives, entrepreneurship support, and leadership development.
